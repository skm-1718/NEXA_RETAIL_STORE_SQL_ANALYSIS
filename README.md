# NEXA_RETAIL_STORE_SQL_ANALYSIS

Analyzed NexaRetail’s retail SQL database to solve 10 business problems related to revenue, store performance, category efficiency, target achievement, declining products, and revenue concentration. Delivered SQL queries, result analysis, and business recommendations to support data-driven decision-making.

# PROJECT OBJECTIVE

•	Analyze NexaRetail’s 2023 retail transaction database using SQL to answer 10 business questions related to revenue, store performance, product performance, sales targets, and growth trends.

•	Generate precise, data-backed insights for business decision-making rather than building a visual dashboard.
Project Goals

•	Measure overall revenue performance across quarters and months.

•	Identify top-performing stores, cities, and product categories.

•	Evaluate store target achievement and store-type productivity.

•	Detect declining products and assess revenue concentration risk.

•	Recommend actions to improve sales, assortment, and operational focus.

# BUSINESS QUESTIONS, RESULTS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

Q1. What is the total revenue for 2023, broken down by quarter?

Result:

Q1 ₹52,566,890 

Q2 ₹51,705,690 

Q3 ₹52,938,370 

Q4 ₹52,551,140

Recommendation: Revenue remained stable across all four quarters, with Q3 being the strongest quarter. NexaRetail can use this pattern to plan promotions, inventory, and staffing around peak-performing periods.

Q2. Which are the top 5 stores by total revenue? Are they all in the same city?

Result: Top 5 stores:

Pawar General (Nagpur), 

More Supermarket Viman Nagar (Surat), 

Jadhav Store (Nagpur), 

D-Mart Kothrud (Surat), 

Mehta Kirana (Ahmedabad).

Recommendation: Top-performing stores are spread across multiple cities rather than concentrated in one market, indicating a healthy geographic revenue spread. Management should study the common success factors of these stores and replicate them in lower-performing locations.

Q3. Which product category has the highest revenue per transaction?

Result: Snacks – ₹7,443.82 revenue per transaction.

Recommendation: The Snacks category generates the highest value per customer transaction. NexaRetail should prioritize this category for merchandising, bundling, and promotional campaigns to lift basket value.

Q4. How many stores met their monthly target in at least 8 of 12 months?

Result: 3 stores met their target in at least 8 months.

Recommendation: Only a small number of stores consistently met targets, indicating uneven store performance. Management should analyze what these high-performing stores are doing differently and transfer those practices to underperforming stores.

Q5. What is the month-over-month revenue growth rate? Which month had the sharpest drop?

Result: Sharpest revenue decline occurred in March 2023 at -14.65% month-over-month.

Recommendation: March 2023 marks the most significant slowdown in revenue momentum. NexaRetail should investigate whether this decline was caused by seasonality, stock shortages, weak promotions, or poor store execution.

Q6. Which city has the highest average basket size? Which has the lowest?

Result: Highest average basket size city: Surat. Lowest average basket size city: Mumbai.

Recommendation: Surat demonstrates stronger customer spending per order, while Mumbai shows the weakest basket value. NexaRetail should explore upselling, product bundling, and assortment optimization in low basket-size cities.

Q7. Are Kirana stores or Supermarkets generating more revenue per store?

Result: 

Kirana: ₹5,234,148.33 per store 

Supermarket: ₹5,273,764.00 per store.

Recommendation: Supermarkets slightly outperform Kirana stores in revenue per store. This suggests that supermarket format may be marginally more productive and can be prioritized in future expansion and merchandising decisions.

Q8. Which 3 products have declining monthly sales volume (units, not revenue)?

Result: NexaGold Sunflower Oil 5L (8 decline months), NexaCrunch Mixture 200g (7), NexaGold Moong Dal 1kg (7)

Recommendation: These products show repeated volume decline and may be losing relevance due to pricing, competition, or demand shifts. NexaRetail should review pricing, visibility, promotions, and possible SKU rationalization for these items.

Q9. What percentage of total revenue comes from the top 10% of stores?

Result: 10.92% of total revenue comes from the top 10% of stores.

Recommendation: Revenue is not excessively concentrated in a few stores, which indicates a relatively balanced store network. However, NexaRetail should still nurture top-performing stores while improving mid-tier stores to strengthen resilience.

Q10. If NexaRetail discontinued its bottom 3 products by revenue, how much revenue would be lost as a percentage of total?

Result: Revenue loss would be 1.92% of total revenue.

Recommendation: The bottom 3 products contribute very little to total revenue, so discontinuing them could simplify inventory and free working capital with minimal sales impact, provided they are not strategically important for customer retention.
